What is Anemia?

Anemia is a condition in which your blood lacks enough healthy red blood cells or hemoglobin. Hemoglobin is the part of red blood cells that binds oxygen. If you have anemia, your body does not get enough oxygen-rich blood.

Common Symptoms:

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Pale or yellowish skin
  • Irregular heartbeats
  • Shortness of breath
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness

Causes of Anemia:

Anemia can be caused by blood loss, decreased red blood cell production, or increased red blood cell destruction. Iron deficiency is the most common cause.
